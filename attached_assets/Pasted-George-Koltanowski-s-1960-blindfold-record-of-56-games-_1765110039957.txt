Miscellanea and Unusual FactsChess has an Elo rating system, which was invented by Hungarian-American physicist Arpad Elo and is now used in many other competitive games and sports.Some chess professionals employ secondants (assistant players or coaches) during major tournaments to help with opening preparation and game analysis.Magnus Carlsen once played ten consecutive checkmates in a single tournament, showcasing his tactical accuracy.The World Chess Olympiad, where nations compete in team events, first took place in 1927 and is organized by FIDE.The first mass-market Soviet chess computer, the Electronica IM-01, was produced in 1977.The Knight is the only piece that is not blocked by other pieces and can "jump" over them.The Bishop is sometimes referred to as a "Churchman" due to its mitre-like shape.The "Grob Opening" ($1. g4$) is an eccentric opening sometimes used to intimidate or confuse unprepared opponents.The "Légal Mate" (Mate in 3-5 moves) is one of the oldest known chess traps, named after 18th-century French master Sire de Legal.Vladimir Putin officially designated chess as a required part of the school curriculum in Russia.In the 19th century, players were sometimes known to use live animals (like cats) near the board to try and distract their opponents.In 2018, Grandmaster Hou Yifan became the youngest female player ever to achieve the Grandmaster title at the age of 14 years and 6 months.The "Reti Opening" ($1. Nf3$) is named after the Hypermodern master Richard Réti, who championed indirect central control.Chess boxing is a hybrid sport where competitors alternate between rounds of chess and boxing.The original Indian word for the game, Chaturanga, means "four divisions," referring to the military components represented by the pieces.Pawn storms are aggressive pawn advances, usually on the wing, aimed at weakening the opponent's King safety.
