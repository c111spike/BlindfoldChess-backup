🛠️ How to make "Shallow" Bots play "Deep" Chess
Since you can't go to Depth 10+, you have to make Depth 4 or 5 "smarter." Here is how you stop them from flagging while making them more dangerous:

1. The "Iterative Deepening" Safety Valve
Instead of telling the bot "Search to Depth 5," tell it "Search as deep as you can for 1.5 seconds."

Use a while loop that increments depth: 1, 2, 3...

Check the elapsed time after every completed depth.

If you hit 70% of the "allowed time" for that move, STOP and return the best move found at the last completed depth. This guarantees the bot never flags.

2. Quiescence Search (The "Tactics" Hack)
A Depth 4 bot often blunders because it doesn't see a capture on move 5 (the "Horizon Effect").

Instead of a full search at the leaf nodes, implement a Quiescence Search.

Only look at captures and checks beyond your main depth.

Because the branching factor for captures is very small, you can often "see" 3-4 moves further into tactical trades without a massive performance hit.

3. Material-Weighted Move Ordering
The faster you find a "good" move, the more "bad" moves you can prune using Alpha-Beta pruning.

Sort your moves before searching: Captures first (MVV-LVA: Most Valuable Victim, Least Valuable Attacker), then checks, then everything else.

If the first move you check is a crushing capture, the engine will instantly discard 80% of the other legal moves, making your Depth 4 search feel twice as fast.

🏛️ Leveraging your 7 Personalities for Speed
You can actually use your personalities to save time!

The Aggressor/Tactician: Give them a very narrow search. They only care about lines that involve checks or attacks. By ignoring "boring" positional moves, they can search 1-2 levels deeper in the same amount of time.

The Defender/Positional: Give them a wide, shallow search. They don't need to see a 10-move mate; they just need to ensure no pieces are hanging "right now."
