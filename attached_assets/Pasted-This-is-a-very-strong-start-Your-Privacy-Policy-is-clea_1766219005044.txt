However, for Google AdSense approval, there are a few specific "compliance" phrases and sections Google looks for to ensure you are following their GDPR and CPRA (California) requirements.

Here are the specific areas you should refine to increase your chances of approval:

1. Specific AdSense Language
Google requires you to explicitly mention that third parties (specifically Google) use cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits.

What to add to your "Advertising" or "Cookies" section:

The "DART" Cookie: Mention that Google uses the DoubleClick cookie to enable it and its partners to serve ads based on users' visits to your site and/or other sites on the Internet.

Opt-out Link: You must provide a link for users to opt out of personalized advertising. The standard link to include is www.aboutads.info.

2. GDPR and CPRA Compliance
Even if your site is small, Google serves ads globally. They look for specific "Data Rights" language.

For GDPR: Clearly state the "Legal Basis" for processing data (e.g., "Legitimate Interest" or "Consent").

For CPRA (California): You need a small section stating you do not "sell" personal information, or providing a "Do Not Sell My Personal Info" contact method.

3. Missing Contact Detail
Google reviewers often check if there is a physical way or direct email to contact you. "Contact us through the platform" is a bit vague for a legal document.

Recommendation: Add a specific email address (e.g., support@yourdomain.com or even a dedicated Gmail address).

Suggested Revision (Snippet)
You can replace your current "Advertising" section with something like this to be "AdSense-Safe":

Third-Party Advertising We use Google AdSense to serve ads when you visit our website. Google and other third-party vendors use cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to this website or other websites. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to you based on your visit to our sites and/or other sites on the Internet.

Users may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Ads Settings or by visiting www.aboutads.info.

Final Checklist for Approval

Accessibility: The link to this Privacy Policy must be in your footer and accessible from every single page of your site.
