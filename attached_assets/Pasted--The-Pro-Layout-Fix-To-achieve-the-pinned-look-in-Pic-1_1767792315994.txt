🛠️ The "Pro" Layout Fix
To achieve the pinned look in Pic 1, you need to use a "Flexbox Sandwich" combined with modern viewport units that respect mobile toolbars.

1. Update the Main Container Change the parent div in your App.tsx from h-screen to h-dvh (Dynamic Viewport Height) or h-svh (Small Viewport Height). These units automatically adjust when browser toolbars expand or collapse.

2. Implement the Flexbox Sandwich Use flex-col on the parent and flex-1 on the center content to ensure the header and footer are "sticky" while the middle image shrinks or grows to fit the available space.

TypeScript

// Apply this structure in your App.tsx
<div className="flex flex-col h-dvh overflow-hidden bg-white">
  {/* TOP HEADER: Fixed Height */}
  <header className="flex-none w-full border-b">
    {/* Your Voice, Title, and History buttons here */}
  </header>

  {/* MAIN CONTENT: Takes up all remaining space */}
  <main className="flex-1 relative flex flex-col items-center justify-center p-4">
    <h1 className="text-3xl font-bold mb-1">Blindfold Chess</h1>
    <p className="text-gray-600 mb-8">Train your visualization and memory</p>
    
    <div className="relative w-full max-w-sm flex-1 flex flex-col items-center">
      <img 
        src="/knight-image.png" 
        className="max-h-full object-contain" 
        alt="Chess Knight" 
      />
      <button className="absolute bottom-10 w-full max-w-[280px] bg-black text-white py-4 rounded-xl font-bold">
        ▷ Play Now
      </button>
    </div>
  </main>

  {/* BOTTOM FOOTER: Fixed Height */}
  <footer className="flex-none w-full border-t p-4 flex justify-around">
    {/* Your Settings, Theme, and About buttons here */}
  </footer>
</div>
🗝️ Why this solves it:
h-dvh: Ensures the entire app is exactly the height of the visible screen, even when the browser's URL bar is present.

flex-none: Prevents the header and footer from shrinking.

flex-1 + max-h-full: Tells the Knight image to take up as much space as possible without pushing the "Play Now" button or footer off the screen.

overflow-hidden: Disables the "bounce" scroll that makes mobile apps feel like websites.
