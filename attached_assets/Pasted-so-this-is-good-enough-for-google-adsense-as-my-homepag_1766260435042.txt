Based on your screenshots, the current layout is a User Dashboard, which is excellent for a web application but generally not sufficient as a homepage for Google AdSense approval.

Google AdSense requires a public-facing homepage that "crawlers" can read without logging in. Since your screenshots show a private dashboard (with "Welcome back," Elo ratings, and "Dev Mode"), the AdSense bot will likely see a login screen or a "thin" page and reject the application for "Low Value Content."

Why a Dashboard isn't a "Homepage" for AdSense
Authentication Barrier: AdSense bots cannot log in. If the high-value text is behind a login or on a dashboard, the bot effectively sees an empty site.

Text-to-Code Ratio: A dashboard is heavy on "interactivity" (buttons, stats, grids) but low on "prose" (paragraphs of information). Google needs articles and descriptions to categorize your site for advertisers.

Navigation Requirements: AdSense looks for standard site navigation (About, Contact, Features) on a public root domain (simulchess.com).

The Solution: Create a "Public Front Door"
You should keep your current dashboard exactly as it is for logged-in users, but create a public homepage for everyone else. This public page should act as a "Marketing Site" that explains what SimulChess is.

1. Above the Fold (Hero Section)
Instead of Elo ratings, show a clear headline: "Master Your Over-The-Board Chess Skills." Include a button that says "Join Now" or "Login to Train."

2. Features Grid (The SEO Layer)
Take the "Knight's Tour," "N-Piece Challenge," and "Board Spin" links from your sidebar and turn them into big, beautiful cards in the middle of your homepage.

Each card should have a 2-3 sentence description.

Critical: These cards must link to the Landing Pages we wrote earlier, NOT directly into the game.

3. The "About" and "How it Works" Sections
Include 300-500 words of text on this homepage. Mention "Stockfish analysis," "Visualization training," and "Simultaneous exhibition practice." This is the "meat" that the AdSense bot will eat.

4. The Footer
Keep your current sidebar links (Privacy Policy, Terms, Contact) but put them in a traditional horizontal footer at the bottom of this new public homepage.

Exactly. Keeping the sidebar as a persistent element is a classic SaaS (Software as a Service) design pattern. It provides "Contextual Continuity"—the user never feels lost because the "tools" (the sidebar) are always there, even as the content in the main window changes.

However, to satisfy Google AdSense, you must ensure that when a logged-out user or a Google bot visits your site, the main content area is a High-Value Public Landing Page, not a "Private Dashboard."

1. How to Handle the Sidebar Logic
You want your sidebar to be "Smart." You don't need two different sidebars; you just need to change what is inside it based on whether the user is logged in.

Logged-Out State (The "Guest" View):

Show links to the 8 Landing Pages (e.g., "Blindfold Training", "Knight's Tour").

Show a "Login / Join" button at the top or bottom.

Hide "ELO Ratings," "Game History," or "Personal Stats" (since there is no data to show yet).

Logged-In State (The "Member" View):

Keep the same 8 links.

Show the User Profile, Ratings, and Logout button.

2. The "Public Front Door" Design
While the sidebar stays on the left, the Main Content Area (the big space on the right) must act as your marketing homepage. Use a "Bento Box" or "Grid" layout to keep it looking modern.

What to put in that main space for the public:

Hero Section: A big title like "Build Grandmaster Vision" and a clear "Get Started" button.

The Features Grid: Large, clickable cards for your top modes.

The "SEO Text" Block: 2-3 paragraphs explaining the science of chess memory. This is what Google’s bot will read to approve your AdSense account.
