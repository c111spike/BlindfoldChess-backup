Moving from Master to Grandmaster (2500+ Elo) is the hardest climb in engine development. At this level, it’s no longer about "not blundering"—it's about deep positional understanding and extreme search efficiency.

Here are the 4 technical "pillars" to turn your Master bot into a Grandmaster:

1. The "Tapered" Evaluation (Game Phase Aware)
GMs play differently in the opening than in the endgame. Currently, your bot likely has one set of values. A GM bot needs Phase-Awareness:

The Logic: Assign a "Phase Value" to the game (e.g., Queens are 4 points of "phase," Knights are 1). Total possible = 24.

The Interpolation: Create two evaluation tables: OpeningTable and EndgameTable.

The Math: If Phase is 24, use 100% Opening weights. If Phase is 0, use 100% Endgame weights. In between, linearly interpolate the score.

The Result: The bot will automatically start "clumping" its pawns in the opening but "marching" its King to the center as soon as the pieces are traded off.

2. Null Move Pruning (The "Speed Demon")
To reach "Grandmaster" depth (Depth 14-16) within your time limits, you must prune more aggressively.

The Concept: "If I pass my turn and my position is still so good that my opponent can't even get back to 'even,' then this branch is a waste of time to calculate."

The Benefit: This allows the bot to "skip" entire chunks of the search tree that are clearly winning/losing, effectively doubling your search depth for free.

3. Sophisticated Pawn Structure
At the GM level, pawns are the "soul of chess." Your evaluation should look for:

Passed Pawns: Huge bonus, especially as they get closer to promotion.

Isolated/Doubled Pawns: Small penalties that add up over 10 moves.

King Safety (Pawn Shield): Penalize the bot heavily if it moves the pawns in front of its castled King.

4. Transposition Tables (Zobrist Hashing)
This is the ultimate "Memory" upgrade.

The Problem: In a Depth 10 search, the bot calculates the exact same board position thousands of times via different move orders.

The Solution: Create a Zobrist Hash (a unique ID) for every board state. Store the evaluation of that state in a large hash table.

The Result: If the bot sees a position it already calculated in a different branch, it just "remembers" the answer instantly. This is how engines like Stockfish hit Depth 20+ in seconds.
