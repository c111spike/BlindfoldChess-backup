You’re right to be cautious—"Hard-coded" recaptures are a classic way for bots to fall into "Desperado" traps or "Intermezzo" (in-between) moves. If the opponent sacrifices a Queen but your recapture leads to an immediate checkmate, the bot should ignore the Queen and save the King.

Since your bot is already strong (scoring 1800 against a 2850 is a massive win for a solo-dev engine!), you don't need to overwrite its brain. You just need to fix its "Vision."

Here are three more sophisticated ways to fix this without making the bot "blindly" recapture:

1. The "Quiescence Search" (Standard Engine Fix)
The reason your bot "sacrificed" the Queen is likely the Horizon Effect. It searched to, say, Depth 6. At Depth 5, it saw the Queen trade. At Depth 6, it stopped looking before the recapture happened. To the bot, the Queen just disappeared, and it "guessed" the value was okay.

The Fix: Implement a Quiescence Search. When the bot reaches its max depth, it shouldn't just stop. It should continue searching only capture moves until there are no more captures left (a "quiet" position).

Why it's safe: It won't "auto-recapture" if it's a trap, because the search will continue and see the trap. It only recaptures if the material balance stays healthy at the end of the sequence.

2. Static Exchange Evaluation (SEE)
Instead of a full search, use an SEE algorithm. This is a fast way to "math out" a square. It looks at all attackers and defenders on the Queen's square and determines the final "Swap-off Value."

The Fix: When the bot evaluates the "Queen for Queen" move, the SEE will return 0 (equal trade). If there's a trap, the SEE will return a negative number (e.g., -900 because it loses a Queen and gets nothing).

Why it's safe: It treats the trade as a single unit of logic. If the math doesn't add up, the bot will reject the initial Queen trade offer entirely.

3. Move Ordering (The "Recapture Bonus")
If you don't want to change the search logic, change the Move Ordering.

The Fix: In your Minimax, sort moves so that Recaptures (moves going to the square where the last piece was lost) are searched first.

Why it's safe: This doesn't force a move; it just makes the bot look at the recapture first. Because it looks at it first, it's more likely to find the correct line even at lower depths.

My Recommendation for SimulChess:
Given your current setup, I would go with Quiescence Search. It's the "industry standard" for a reason. It fixes the "Queen Sacrifice" bug permanently while still allowing the bot to spot traps.
