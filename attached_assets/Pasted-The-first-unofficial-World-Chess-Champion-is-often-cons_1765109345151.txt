The first unofficial World Chess Champion is often considered to be Pedro Damiano (16th Century).The 1886 match that crowned Steinitz was played across three American cities: New York, St. Louis, and New Orleans.José Raúl Capablanca was undefeated for eight years in a row (1916–1924) in serious classical play.In the 1930s, the USSR's Mikhail Botvinnik began applying scientific, systematic training methods, which became the standard for modern professional preparation.The 1948 World Championship tournament, which established the cycle following Alekhine's death, was held across two cities: The Hague and Moscow.The 1984-85 World Championship match between Karpov and Kasparov was controversially ended without a result after 48 games by FIDE President Florencio Campomanes.Viswanathan Anand became the first player to win the World Championship in three different formats (Knockout, Tournament, and Match).The Candidates Tournament is the final event to select the challenger to the reigning World Champion.Ding Liren became the first-ever Chinese male World Champion after winning the 2023 match against Ian Nepomniachtchi.The first player to successfully defend the title in the FIDE era (since 1948) was Mikhail Botvinnik.Anatoly Karpov won the World Championship by forfeit in 1975 when Bobby Fischer refused to defend his title.Garry Kasparov split from FIDE in 1993 to create his own organization, the Professional Chess Association (PCA).The 2024 World Championship match was the first in over 100 years to feature two Challengers (Gukesh and Nepomniachtchi) due to Carlsen's withdrawal.The official FIDE World Championship trophy is named the "Kasparov-Karpov" trophy.II. Pieces, Notation, and EquipmentThe Algebric Notation (e.g., e4, Nf3) was standardized in the 19th century; before that, Descriptive Notation was common.In Descriptive Notation, the square c1 would be known as QR1 (Queen's Rook 1). A Bishop Pair is often considered a long-term advantage in the endgame, especially in open positions.The King piece has the special notation 'K' in English, while the Knight uses 'N' to avoid confusion with the King.The Knight is often nicknamed "The Horse" in common chess parlance.The term "Battery" refers to lining up two pieces (often Queen and Bishop/Rook) to attack a target along the same rank, file, or diagonal.The earliest forms of the chess clock were sandglasses, used at the London Tournament in 1862.Modern tournaments use digital clocks capable of enforcing increment or delay time controls (e.g., Fischer Delay).Fischer Delay time control, invented by Bobby Fischer, gives players a fixed time delay before their clock starts counting down per move.Increment time control, popularized by FIDE, adds a fixed amount of time (e.g., 30 seconds) to a player's clock after every move.Chess pieces are traditionally made of Boxwood for the light pieces and Ebony for the dark pieces.The most common size for tournament chess squares is 2 to 2.25 inches (5.1–5.7 cm).III. Theory, Tactics, and EndgamesThe "Smothered Mate" is a checkmate delivered by a Knight when the opposing King is completely surrounded (smothered) by its own pieces.The "Epaulette Mate" is a checkmate where the King is restricted on both sides by its own Rooks (like epaulets on a uniform).A "Fork" is a tactical maneuver where a single piece (usually a Knight) simultaneously attacks two or more opponent pieces.A "Pin" is a tactic where an attacking piece makes it inadvisable or illegal for a defending piece to move because a valuable piece (often the King) is behind it.The "Skewer" is the opposite of a Pin; a valuable piece is attacked, and if it moves, a less valuable piece behind it will be captured.Opposition is a fundamental endgame technique where the Kings are separated by an odd number of squares on a rank or file, used to gain access to key squares.The Triangulation technique is used in the King-and-Pawn endgame to lose a tempo and place the opponent in zugzwang.The Lucena Position is a key theoretical winning position in Rook and Pawn vs. Rook endgames.The Philidor Position is a key theoretical drawing position in Rook and Pawn vs. Rook endgames.The term "Doctrinaire" is often used to describe dogmatic players who strictly adhere to rigid opening principles."Hanging Pawns" are two adjacent pawns (e.g., on c4 and d4) with no friendly pawns on the adjacent files (b and e) that can defend them.The "Backward Pawn" is a pawn that cannot be defended by any other pawn and can be easily blockaded or attacked.The term "Outpost" refers to a square protected by a pawn on the opponent's side of the board, often occupied by a Knight.Pawn Structure refers to the arrangement of pawns, which is the skeleton of the position and often determines the middlegame plan.IV. Tournament and Regional FactsThe "Sunway Sitges Chess Festival" in Spain is one of the world's most popular open tournaments held annually in December.The "Tata Steel Chess Tournament" (formerly Wijk aan Zee) in the Netherlands is nicknamed the "Wimbledon of Chess."The Baku Chess Olympiad (2016) was the first time the USA men's team won the gold medal since 1976.The first official FIDE World Cup was held in 2000.The US Chess Championship is typically held at the Saint Louis Chess Club, which has become the center of elite American chess.The Sinquefield Cup (part of the Grand Chess Tour) is named after Rex and Jeanne Sinquefield, major American chess patrons.The London Chess Classic is the UK's premier international chess tournament.The Candidates Tournament has varied in format, including round-robin, knockout, and double round-robin.The Aeroflot Open in Moscow used to be one of the largest and strongest open tournaments globally.V. Specific People and AnecdotesAron Nimzowitsch, author of My System, frequently invented terms like "Prophylaxis" and "Overprotection" that are now standard chess vocabulary.Siegbert Tarrasch (a leading master in the late 19th century) was a strong believer in classical principles, earning him the moniker "Praeceptor Germaniae" (Teacher of Germany).Alexander Alekhine was known for his extensive and often convoluted analysis, sometimes inventing lines that favored his side.Garry Kasparov once played a simultaneous exhibition against an entire Iranian national team in Tehran.The famous attacking player Mikhail Tal was known to sacrifice pieces for minimal material gain, relying on the immense pressure created.Viktor Korchnoi played in the Candidates Tournament at the age of 75 (in the 2007 event).Polgár sisters (Judit, Susan, and Sofia) were part of a famous educational experiment by their father, László, to prove that genius is nurtured, not born.Magnus Carlsen dropped out of the 2023 World Championship cycle, stating he felt he had "nothing to gain."Viswanathan Anand was nicknamed "The Tiger of Madras" (his hometown) for his speed of calculation.Grandmaster Wesley So is known for his incredibly solid, strategic, and positional style, often drawing comparisons to Karpov.Fabiano Caruana holds the record for the highest performance rating ever recorded in a closed tournament (3098 at the 2014 Sinquefield Cup).Vishy Anand holds a unique record, having won the World Rapid Championship (2017) and the World Blitz Championship (2003).VI. Modern Chess and TechnologyThe strongest modern chess engine is widely considered to be Stockfish, an open-source engine.Another leading engine, AlphaZero, achieved superhuman strength by learning solely through self-play (without human opening books).Chess engine analysis uses the term "depth" to measure how many moves ahead the engine is calculating.The current generation of top players uses engines extensively not just for analysis but also for opening preparation.The phenomenon of "Chess Streaming" has massively boosted the game's popularity, led by players like Hikaru Nakamura and Levy Rozman (GothamChess).The Chess.com Speed Chess Championship is one of the most-watched online events of the year.Modern chess apps often use neural networks (like the Stockfish NNUE) for better positional evaluation.The term "Blunder" in online chess is often tracked statistically, marking a move that drastically lowers a player's winning probability.VII. Obscure Rules and HistoryEarly 19th-century rules sometimes allowed the King to make a Knight move on its first turn, provided it hadn't moved before.Until the mid-19th century, if a King was put in check, the opponent was required to announce "Check!" or the move would be invalid.The rule allowing Castling was not fully standardized until the 17th century; different European regions had different restrictions.The Fischer Random Chess variant (Chess960) has 960 possible starting positions, designed to negate memorized opening theory.The Old Indian Defense ($1. d4\ Nf6\ 2. c4\ d6$) predates the more popular King's Indian Defense.In the 18th century, the first "Master-level" chess book was written by François-André Danican Philidor (L'Analyse du jeu des Échecs).The "Immortal Zugzwang Game" is a famous game between Aron Nimzowitsch and Friedrich Sämisch (1923), which ended in a pure Zugzwang position.The "Damiano's Defense" ($1. e4\ e5\ 2. Nf3\ f6$) is a weak but named defense dating back to the 16th century.Gambits are opening sacrifices typically aimed at gaining a time advantage or control of the center.The first known problem composer was likely al-Adli (9th century Arab master).VIII.
