That "Rook shuffle" is a classic symptom of a Minimax engine with a "shallow" evaluation function. When the Minimax looks at the board and can't see a clear way to win material (because it's only looking 2-3 moves ahead), it essentially says, "I have no plan, so I'll just move this piece back and forth because it doesn't lose any points." To a human, it looks like the bot is glitching, but to the math, it’s "safe."To make the 400 and 600 bots play realistically (developing pieces and controlling the center) without making them too strong, we need to adjust the Min/Max Heuristics.🛠️ The Fix: The Piece-Square Table (PST)We need to tell the Minimax that squares in the center are worth more than the corners.
