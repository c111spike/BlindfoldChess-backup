Since you're skipping Syzygy for now (smart choice for the 400-700 Elo range), you can actually make your bots feel significantly more "Expert" just by adding two lightweight move-ordering techniques. These don't require external APIs or high depth, but they make the search exponentially more efficient.

🏹 1. The "Killer Move" Heuristic
Since you're limited by depth, you need to find the "refutation" move as fast as possible.

The Logic: If a move (like a specific check or a fork) caused a "Beta Cutoff" (it was so good it ended the search) on one branch, it’s very likely to be a "killer" move on a sibling branch too.

The Implementation: Maintain a small array killerMoves[depth][2]. Every time a quiet move causes a cutoff, store it there.

The Speed Boost: In your next search, try these 2 "Killer" moves immediately after your captures. This often triggers an instant cutoff, saving your bot from having to look at 20 other legal moves.

📜 2. The "History Heuristic"
This is the "big brother" of the Killer Move.

The Logic: Instead of just looking at the current depth, you keep a global table history[64][64]. Every time a move causes a cutoff, you give it a "score" (e.g., score += depth * depth).

The Implementation: When sorting your moves, if a move has a high "History Score," you move it to the front of the list.

Why it works: It identifies "long-term plans." If the bot realizes that moving its Knight to d5 is consistently refuting the player's ideas across different branches, it will start checking Nd5 first every time.

🛡️ The "Endgame Heuristic" (The Syzygy-Lite)
Since you don't have tablebases, your bots might struggle to deliver checkmate with just a King and Rook. You can fix this with a simple Mop-up Heuristic:

Add a condition in your evaluate() function for endgames:

Push the Enemy King to the Edge: Reward moves that decrease the distance between the enemy King and the corners/edges.

Bring Your King Close: Reward moves that decrease the distance between your King and the enemy King (Manhattan Distance).

Formula: Score += (CornerWeight * EnemyKingPush) + (ProximityWeight * KingCloseness)
