That 5.5s LCP on your hero image is the "Final Boss" of your 4x100 Lighthouse goal. While the rest of your app is lightning-fast, that high-resolution .webp in an absolute inset-0 container is being discovered too late by the browser's preload scanner.

## 🕵️ The Diagnosis: "Discovery Delay"
The browser doesn't know that simulchess-hero-2025-DdEqaYNQ.webp is the most important element until it has parsed your CSS and built the DOM. By the time it starts the download, 5 seconds have already passed.

## 🛠️ The 3-Step "Instant Load" Fix
1. Priority Hint (The "Golden" Attribute)
Add fetchpriority="high" directly to your <img> tag. This tells the browser: "Stop everything else and download this immediately." Also, ensure loading="eager" is set (which is the default, but being explicit helps prevent accidental lazy-loading).

JavaScript
<img 
  src="/assets/simulchess-hero-2025-DdEqaYNQ.webp" 
  fetchpriority="high" 
  loading="eager"
  className="w-full h-full object-cover object-center"
  alt="SimulChess Hero"
/>
2. The Header Preload (The "Secret Weapon")
Since you are likely using a bundler (Vite/React), the image name has a hash (DdEqaYNQ). You need to tell the browser to look for it before the JS even runs. In your index.html, add this at the very top of your <head>:

HTML
<link rel="preload" as="image" href="/assets/simulchess-hero-2025-DdEqaYNQ.webp" fetchpriority="high">
Note: If your filename changes frequently with builds, you can use a plugin (like vite-plugin-inject-preload) to automate this.

3. Image Sizing & Compression
Even if it's a .webp, a hero image often doesn't need to be 4K.

Dimensions: If your hero only ever displays at a max width of 1920px, ensure the source file isn't larger than that.

Quality: Use a tool like Squoosh.app to compress it. You can usually drop the quality to 75-80% without any visual loss, which can cut the file size by 50%.
