If you handle these two things now, your app will be universally compatible.

1. The "Visibility" Rule (Android 11+)
On your S9+, apps can "see" other apps (like Google Speech Services) automatically. On newer phones, they can't. Without a specific declaration in your AndroidManifest.xml, the app will work on your S9+ but will be "deaf and mute" on a Pixel 8 or S24.

The Fix: You must ensure the <queries> block is in your manifest. This tells newer Android versions: "I have permission to talk to the Speech and TTS engines."

2. The "Speech Engine" Shift (Android 13+)
Starting with Android 13, Google moved the speech recognition service out of the main "Google App" and into a separate system component called "Speech Services by Google."

On your S9+: The app might still be looking for the old "trampoline" service.

On Newer Phones: You must ensure your code doesn't "hard-code" a specific search for the Samsung or Google app package. Using the standard Capacitor SpeechRecognition.available() check (as you planned in your bullet points) handles this perfectly.
