. History and OriginOrigin: Chess originated in India around the 6th century AD. The earliest known form was called Chaturanga, which means "four divisions" (infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots, represented by the modern pawn, knight, bishop, and rook).The Name: The word "Chess" comes from the Persian word "shāh," meaning "king."Checkmate: The word "checkmate" comes from the Persian phrase "shāh māt," meaning "the king is helpless" or "the king is dead."Introduction to Europe: The game spread through Persia and the Islamic world, arriving in Europe (specifically Spain and Italy) around the 10th century.Queen's Power: The pieces gained their modern movement rules (especially the powerful Queen and Bishop) in the late 15th century, during the Renaissance. This version is sometimes called "Queen's Chess" or "Mad Queen Chess."First World Champion: The first official World Chess Champion was Wilhelm Steinitz, who claimed the title in 1886.2. Mathematics and PossibilityShannon Number: The Shannon Number is an estimate of the complexity of chess, calculated as $10^{120}$. This represents the estimated number of possible unique games.Game Length: The longest tournament game ever played was between Ivan Nikolić and Goran Arsović in 1989, lasting 20 hours and 15 minutes over 269 moves (ending in a draw).No Draws Rule (Record): A rule existed that prohibited draws before move 50. The longest game played under this rule lasted 193 moves between Karpov and Kaidanov (1993).Starting Moves: From the initial position, White has 20 possible first moves. After the first pair of moves, there are 400 possible positions.Average Game: The average chess game lasts about 40 moves.Longest Forced Mate: The longest known forced mate in a composed problem is over 500 moves, though in practical play, the 50-move rule usually forces a draw long before that.3. Pieces and RulesFifty-Move Rule: If 50 moves pass without either a pawn move or a capture, a player may claim a draw. This prevents extremely long, tedious endgames where no progress is being made.Promotion: A pawn is the only piece that changes identity. When a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, it must be promoted to a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight (typically a Queen).En Passant: This is a special pawn capture rule invented in the 15th century. It allows a pawn to capture an opponent's pawn that has moved two squares forward on its first move, treating it as if it had only moved one square.Castling: This is the only move in chess where two pieces (King and Rook) move simultaneously, and the only time a King moves two squares.4. Modern and Cultural FactsFIDE: The global governing body for chess is the Fédération Internationale des Échecs (FIDE), founded in Paris on July 20, 1924. This date is now celebrated as International Chess Day.Grandmaster Title: The Grandmaster (GM) title is the highest title a chess player can achieve, awarded by FIDE.Youngest GM: The youngest player ever to earn the Grandmaster title is Abhimanyu Mishra (USA), who achieved the title at the age of 12 years, 4 months, and 25 days in 2021.Highest Rating: The highest classical Elo rating ever achieved is 2882, held by Magnus Carlsen (Norway).Longest Reign: Emanuel Lasker (2nd World Champion) held the title for the longest period: 27 years (1894–1921).Gender: Judit Polgár (Hungary) is widely considered the strongest female player of all time and is the only woman to have broken the 2700 Elo barrier and played for the World Championship title.Computer Chess: In 1997, the IBM computer Deep Blue became the first machine to defeat a reigning World Champion (Garry Kasparov) in a classical match format.5. Chess in SpaceFirst Game in Space: The first game of chess played in space occurred in 1970 between the crew of the Soviet spacecraft Soyuz 9 (Andrian Nikolayev and Vitaly Sevastyanov) and a team on Earth (Viktor Gorbatko and Nikolay Kamanin). It ended in a draw.
