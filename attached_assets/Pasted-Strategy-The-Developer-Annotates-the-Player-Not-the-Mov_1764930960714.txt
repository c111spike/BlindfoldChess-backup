Strategy: The Developer Annotates the Player, Not the Move
You must completely delegate the "what" (the move) to the client-side Stockfish, and focus your server-side data (the "who, when, and how") on the player's psychology and time management.

For a 2400 ELO player, the Stockfish evaluation bar is redundant. Your analysis provides the data they can't get anywhere else: a Scouting Report on their own competitive fitness.

Here is how you provide indispensable value to a Grandmaster without out-playing them:

1. The Principle of Non-Interference (The GM Rule)
Never use your custom annotations to suggest a better move. Let the GM's own client-side Stockfish do that.

Your custom annotations should only ask a diagnostic question that guides the player's own high-level analysis (Source 2.5).

2. The High-Value Annotations for Strong Players
Your server-side annotations become focused, data-driven markers placed over the Stockfish output.

Efficiency Factor (EF) Fluctuation,"Points out moves where their time-per-move suddenly diverged from their game average, revealing either anxiety (rushing) or confusion (over-spending).",You measure their resource allocation. They are master calculators; you tell them when they calculated with low efficiency.
