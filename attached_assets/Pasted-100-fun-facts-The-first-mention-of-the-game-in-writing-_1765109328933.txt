100 fun facts
The first mention of the game in writing in the West was in a Spanish manuscript in the 13th century.The modern, two-square pawn move on the first turn was first introduced in Spain in 1280 to speed up the game.The alternating light and dark squares on the chessboard, as we know it today, first appeared in Europe around 1090.Before the modern Queen (c. 15th century), the piece was called the Vizier (or Farzin/Fers), and it only moved one square diagonally.In early versions of chess (Shatranj), stalemate was considered a win for the stalemated player.The practice of White moving first was not standardized in international competition until 1889. Before that, the choice was often decided by chance.The Folding Chessboard was allegedly invented by a chess-playing priest in 1125 so he could hide it from the Church (which forbade priests from playing).The Rook piece was originally a chariot (Ratha in Sanskrit) and later a Rukh (Persian for chariot/tower), leading to the "castle" image.In French, the Bishop is called Le Fou, meaning "The Fool," a name derived from a misinterpretation of the Arabic word for elephant (al-fil).The English Opening ($1. c4$) was named for English master Howard Staunton, who used it frequently in the 19th century.The term "Rookie" (for a beginner) is sometimes traced back to the Rook piece, which is often the last piece to enter the game.The Sicilian Defense ($1. e4\ c5$) is named after the island of Sicily, though its association with the region is tenuous.The "Immortal Game" (Anderssen vs. Kieseritzky, 1851) was played in London during a break at the world's first international tournament.The Black Death (1347–1351) is thought to have briefly reduced the popularity of chess in Europe due to social upheaval.In some ancient versions, players could win by simply capturing all of the opponent's pieces (leaving a "bare king") without a checkmate.II. Rules, Records, and NumbersThe longest theoretical chess game possible, constrained by FIDE rules, is estimated to be around 5,949 moves.The number of possible ways of playing the first four moves for both sides is over 318 billion.There are 400 possible board positions after one move by White and one move by Black ($1. e4\ e5$).There are over 122 million unique ways to complete a Knight's Tour (a path a knight takes to visit every square exactly once).It is theoretically possible to have nine Queens or up to ten Rooks, Knights, or Bishops of the same color on the board simultaneously (through pawn promotion).The "Under-promotion" to a piece other than a Queen is rare but is most often a Knight to deliver an immediate checkmate or fork.The earliest known example of a Castling move occurring late in a game was in 1966, on move 46.In the 18th century, the rule often required a pawn to only be promoted to a piece already captured by the opponent.Chess clocks were first used in London in the 1862 tournament, initially using hourglasses.The "touch-move" rule (if you touch a piece, you must move it) has been a standard rule in chess for centuries.The Threefold Repetition rule (claiming a draw if the same position occurs three times) was sometimes required to be six repetitions in earlier rule sets.The first book printed in English by William Caxton in 1474, "The Game and Playe of the Chesse," was the second book ever printed in the English language.The "Immortal Draw" is the name given to the 1872 game between Carl Hamppe and Philipp Meitner, famous for its wild sacrifices leading to a forced draw by repetition.The Fastest Checkmate possible in chess is in two moves (Fool's Mate): $1. f3\ e5\ 2. g4\ Qh4\#$.The highest-rated player ever to have the initials "A.K." is Anatoly Karpov.III. Famous Players and AnecdotesEmanuel Lasker (2nd World Champion) retained his title for the longest time: 26 years and 337 days.Lasker was also a distinguished mathematician and philosopher, known for his pragmatic and psychological approach to the game.Garry Kasparov became the youngest undisputed World Champion ever in 1985 at the age of 22 years and 210 days.The chess scene in the James Bond novel From Russia With Love features a game said to be based on an actual 1930 match.The "Game of the Century" was a match played by 13-year-old Bobby Fischer against Donald Byrne in 1956, featuring a famous Queen sacrifice.During the 1972 Fischer–Spassky match, the Soviet team claimed Fischer was trying to distract Spassky using electronic devices planted in his chair. The chair was inspected and nothing was found.Akiba Rubinstein was a brilliant Russian Grandmaster known for his endgame technique, despite never winning the World Championship.Paul Morphy (19th century American master) traveled the world playing without demanding money, only seeking intellectual challenge.World War II code breakers at Bletchley Park included British chess masters Harry Golombek, Stuart Milner-Barry, and C. H. O'D. Alexander.The 1973 Cleveland Chess Tournament was raided by police, who arrested the director and confiscated the chess sets, claiming they were "gambling devices" due to the cash prizes.Vladimir Nabokov, author of Lolita, wrote the novel The Luzhin Defense, which is based on the tragic life of a chess grandmaster.Fidel Castro was a passionate chess player and hosted numerous international tournaments in Cuba.Grandmaster Miguel Najdorf, namesake of the Sicilian Najdorf, was one of the first to give successful blindfold chess exhibitions.Frank Marshall (U.S. Champion for 30 years) was the first American to defeat a Soviet player in an international tournament in 1924.George Koltanowski set a record by playing 56 games blindfolded simultaneously in 1960.IV. Piece Nicknames and Specific TermsZugzwang (German for "compulsion to move") is a position where any legal move a player makes will worsen their position.Zwischenzug (German for "intermediate move") is a tactic where a player inserts an unexpected check or threat before a planned sequence.Desperado is a piece that is doomed to be captured but goes on a rampage, capturing other pieces or checks before its demise to gain an advantage.The Bishop is called "The Runner" (Läufer) in German.In Russian, the Rook is called "Ladya," meaning a boat or ship.The King and Queen pieces are sometimes called the "Royalty" or "Major Pieces."The Knight and Bishop pieces are sometimes called the "Minor Pieces" or "Minors."A "Swindler" is a player known for turning hopelessly lost positions into draws or wins using tricks and traps.The phrase "The Queen on her own color" is a common mnemonic device used to remember the correct starting position of the Queen (White Queen on the d1 white square, Black Queen on the d8 black square).The King and Rook are the only pieces that can participate in the special move Castling.V. Chess in Pop Culture and ArtThe 1957 film "The Seventh Seal" features a famous scene where a medieval knight plays a game of chess against Death.The character Rick Blaine in the 1942 movie Casablanca is seen analyzing a chess game; actor Humphrey Bogart was an avid chess player in real life.The first film to ever feature a chess theme was the British one-minute comedy "A Chess Dispute," released in 1903.The alien invasion in the movie "Independence Day" (1996) is compared to a chess game by the main character, David Levinson.The 1993 film "Searching for Bobby Fischer" is based on the life of chess prodigy Josh Waitzkin.The 1968 film "2001: A Space Odyssey" features a famous chess game played between the computer HAL 9000 and astronaut Frank Poole.Professor X and Magneto are frequently shown playing chess together in the X-Men films, symbolizing their deep strategic rivalry.In "Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone," the main trio must defeat a giant, animated chess set. The sequence is based on a real game (Petrosian vs. Larsen, 1966).The popular Netflix show "The Queen's Gambit" is based on the 1983 novel of the same name by Walter Tevis.Vladimir Lenin was a known fan of chess and was often photographed playing the game while in exile.VI. Blindfold and Simultaneous ChessSimultaneous Exhibition (Simul): A master plays against multiple opponents at the same time.The record for the most simultaneous games played is over 600, set by Grandmaster Alireza Firouzja in 2022 (non-blindfold).The concept of Blindfold Chess originated in the Middle East, with records dating back to the 11th century.Alexander Alekhine (4th World Champion) was famous for his blindfold exhibitions, setting a world record in 1937 by playing 32 games simultaneously.George Koltanowski's 1960 blindfold record of 56 games required him to memorize the precise location and possible moves of 1,792 pieces.VII. Miscellanea and Unusual FactsChess has an Elo rating system, which was invented by Hungarian-American physicist Arpad Elo and is now used in many other competitive games and sports.Some chess professionals employ secondants (assistant players or coaches) during major tournaments to help with opening preparation and game analysis.Magnus Carlsen once played ten consecutive checkmates in a single tournament, showcasing his tactical accuracy.The World Chess Olympiad, where nations compete in team events, first took place in 1927 and is organized by FIDE.The first mass-market Soviet chess computer, the Electronica IM-01, was produced in 1977.The Knight is the only piece that is not blocked by other pieces and can "jump" over them.The Bishop is sometimes referred to as a "Churchman" due to its mitre-like shape.The "Grob Opening" ($1. g4$) is an eccentric opening sometimes used to intimidate or confuse unprepared opponents.The "Légal Mate" (Mate in 3-5 moves) is one of the oldest known chess traps, named after 18th-century French master Sire de Legal.Vladimir Putin officially designated chess as a required part of the school curriculum in Russia.In the 19th century, players were sometimes known to use live animals (like cats) near the board to try and distract their opponents.In 2018, Grandmaster Hou Yifan became the youngest female player ever to achieve the Grandmaster title at the age of 14 years and 6 months.The "Reti Opening" ($1. Nf3$) is named after the Hypermodern master Richard Réti, who championed indirect central control.Chess boxing is a hybrid sport where competitors alternate between rounds of chess and boxing.The original Indian word for the game, Chaturanga, means "four divisions," referring to the military components represented by the pieces.Pawn storms are aggressive pawn advances, usually on the wing, aimed at weakening the opponent's King safety.A famous quote by Emanuel Lasker is: "When you see a good move, look for a better one."The "desperate check" is a common tactic where a player gives a seemingly useless check, only to repeat the position for a draw or win material.The King is actually the weakest piece on the board in terms of movement power, moving only one square at a time.Promotion is not limited to pieces previously captured; if all your original pieces are on the board, you can still promote a pawn to a Queen, resulting in two or more Queens.The term "zwischenzug" (intermediate move) is often applied to a check or forcing move that changes the anticipated order of a planned combination.The Sicilian Defense is the most popular response by Black to $1. e4$ at the master level.The Queen's Gambit is the oldest opening that is still considered fully sound and is popular at the Grandmaster level.The King's Gambit ($1. e4\ e5\ 2. f4$) was the opening of choice during the "Romantic Era" of chess (18th-19th centuries).Queen sacrifices are so rare and brilliant that when they occur, they are often highlighted as moments of genius.The World Computer Chess Championship is a separate tournament where computer programs compete against each other.The Russian School of Chess, originating in the Soviet era, emphasized state support, scientific analysis, and systematic training.Correspondence Chess involves players taking days or weeks for a single move, often using tools and databases for deep analysis.The official FIDE Master (FM) title is ranked below the International Master (IM) and Grandmaster (GM) titles.Chess pieces are often given symbolic point values for comparison: Pawn (1), Knight/Bishop (3), Rook (5), Queen (9), and King (Infinite).
