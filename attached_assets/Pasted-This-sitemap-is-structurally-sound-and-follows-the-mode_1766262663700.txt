1. The "Priority" and "Changefreq" Reality
In 2025, Google officially ignores the <priority> and <changefreq> tags. They won't hurt your site, but Google’s bots now determine crawl frequency based on how often they actually detect meaningful content changes.

Action: You can keep them for other search engines (like Bing), but don't rely on them to "force" Google to crawl faster.

2. The Power of <lastmod>
Unlike priority, Google does pay attention to the <lastmod> tag.

Action: Ensure this date is in the W3C Datetime format (YYYY-MM-DD). Since you are launching now, having them all set to 2025-12-20 is perfect as it signals "fresh content".

3. Critical: The "Deep Link" Strategy
You have listed both the SEO Landing Pages (e.g., /blindfold-chess-training) and the Training Pages (e.g., /standard).

Warning: If the /standard or /otb pages are behind a login or are "thin" (just a game board with no text), Google might choose not to index them.

Strategy: Make sure your SEO Landing Pages have a clear, large button that links to the corresponding Training Mode. This "internal link" tells Google that the training tool is a high-value destination, even if it has less text.
