For a chess platform where users are capturing board states, moves, or chat text, you want a balance between clarity (so you can read the moves/text) and performance (so your Replit storage and admin panel load quickly).

Recommended Dimensions and File Size
Attribute	Recommended Setting	Why?
Resolution	1280 × 720 (720p) or 1920 × 1080 (1080p)	Standard "HD" allows you to clearly read coordinates and chat logs without taking up massive space.
Max File Size	1 MB to 2 MB	Most chess screenshots are simple shapes and colors; anything over 2 MB is usually unoptimized and will slow down your Admin dashboard.
File Format	WebP or JPEG	WebP is the modern standard for the web (30% smaller than JPEG). PNG is great for text clarity but can get very large if the user has a 4K monitor.
Strategic Recommendation: "Auto-Shrink" on Upload
Don't trust users to upload small files! A user on a Retina MacBook or a 4K monitor might take a screenshot that is 10 MB and 4000×3000 pixels.

I recommend implementing a "Frontend Compressor" before the file even hits your server:

Client-side Resize: If the image is wider than 1920px, scale it down to 1920px width.

Compression: Use a library like browser-image-compression to force the image under 1 MB.

Admin Preview: In your Admin panel, show a small thumbnail (e.g., 200px wide) that only expands to full size when clicked. This ensures your "Reports" page doesn't crash your browser if there are 50 images to load.

Why this matters for your Future Chat Update
When you add chat, users will send images much more frequently than they file reports.

Thumbnails are key: For chat, you definitely want to store a "thumbnail version" and a "full version."

Security: Ensure your upload logic strips EXIF data (metadata like GPS location or device name) to protect your users' privacy.

The "Admin Evidence" View
Since you are the admin, you need to see the "Who, What, and Where."

Recommendation: When viewing a screenshot in a report, overlay the Timestamp and User ID on the image record. This prevents users from trying to submit "old" screenshots as "new" evidence.
