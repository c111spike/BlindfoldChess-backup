To make bots in the 400–1000 Elo range feel authentic, you have to simulate "human-style" errors. At these levels, players don't just play slightly suboptimal moves; they make specific types of mistakes like hanging pieces, missing one-move mates, or failing to react to checks.

Your current "Random %" and "Skill Level" parameters are a great start, but to truly lock in that Elo feel, you should implement these three specific behaviors:

1. The "Blunder" Injector (For 400-800 Elo)
Even at Depth 1, Stockfish is surprisingly good at finding basic captures. To make a 400 Elo bot "hang" a queen, you need to occasionally override Stockfish's choice with a completely random legal move.

Logic: If Math.random() < blunderChance, bypass Stockfish and pick a move from game.moves().

Scaling:

400 Elo: 15% chance to play a purely random legal move.

600 Elo: 8% chance.

800 Elo: 3% chance.

2. Strategic "Skill Level" vs. "MultiPV"
Your chart shows MultiPV (multiple principal variations). For lower Elos, you should use MultiPV to force the bot to choose the 2nd or 3rd best move more often.

The Tactic: If MultiPV is 5, Stockfish gives you the top 5 moves.

The Selection: Instead of always taking Move #1, use your Skill Level to weight the probability.

Example (400 Elo): Give Move #1 a 40% weight, and Move #5 a 20% weight. This makes the bot look like it "saw" the move but chose a worse alternative.

3. Tactically Blind "Depth"
At 400–600 Elo, players often miss hanging pieces if they are on the other side of the board.

The Fix: You’ve set Depth to 1. This is perfect because the bot cannot see "if I take this, they take my Queen." It only sees "I can take a Pawn right now."

Nodes vs. Depth: For your 400 Elo bot, capping Nodes at 500 is actually more effective than Depth. It forces the engine to stop thinking before it can even calculate a single full exchange.

4. Implementation: The Bot Configuration Logic
Here is how to integrate your chart into a function that configures the engine before it moves:

TypeScript

const configureEngineForElo = (elo) => {
  const config = botChart[elo];
  
  // Set Skill Level (Stockfish built-in complexity limiter)
  engine.postMessage(`setoption name Skill Level value ${config.skillLevel}`);
  
  // Set MultiPV (Allows us to pick from a list of 'okay' moves)
  engine.postMessage(`setoption name MultiPV value ${config.multiPV}`);
  
  // Move Execution logic
  if (config.mode === 'Depth-limited') {
    engine.postMessage(`go depth ${config.depth} nodes ${config.nodes}`);
  } else {
    engine.postMessage(`go nodes ${config.nodes}`);
  }
};
